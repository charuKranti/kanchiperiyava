PIDI ARISI THITTAM

PIDI ARISI THITTAM

During the golden period when people could witness the Sarveshwaran, prathyaksha Parameshwaran Shri Shri Shri Mahaperiyava , there was a huge gathering at Siva Vishnu Temple, T.Nagar in Chennai on the 10th of March 1966 to listen to the discourse of His Holiness.

Jothi swaroopa Shri Mahaperiyava is emanating golden light like a early morning sun blessing the devotees with his merciful eyes. ". In that upanyasam Shri Periyava guided and insisted everyone with a simple margam ( way of life) to get rid of our sins , easy way to please the divine and lead a peaceful and happy life. Periyava's advices are always simple yet powerful dharma kainkaryams. One among it was PIDI ARISI THITTAM. It was initiated by periyava during that upanyasam.

What is PIDI ARISI THITTAM?

For those who are not aware of it, Periyava gave a easy maargam for the house wives to participate in this dharma kainkaryam. He requested each and every house hold to set aside a handful of rice along with few coins everyday in a container or a bag while they cook for their family. Volunteers would come and collect once/ twice a month and will distribute the same to the patashalas/ temples/ poor and the needy. Why did he requested to add few coins everyday? .. to buy the groceries like dhal, oil,spices and vegetables.

In essence, every housewife and therefore her family participated in feeding the poor and the needy . Thus they become the recipients of their blessings and that of Mahaswami too.

Taking cue from the above message of Shri Periyava, we have started a scheme to contribute INR 1001/- and USD 100$/- monthly per family exclusively to Vedic Patasalas/ Vedic Gurukulams and Vaidheekas towards Akshadai. Those who are unable to contribute the above mentioned money can do it as much as you can.

When you offer Akshadai (rice) to a vaideeka brahman and he gets to eat it and digest the same chanting veda manthram, 21 generations of the family that donated will be blessed says SHRI PERIYAVA.

The contributions will be consolidated and donated to the Veda Patashalas under the auspices of Kanchi Kamakoti Peetam.
