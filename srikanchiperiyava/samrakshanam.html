GHO SAMRAKSHANAM

MAHA PERIYAVA ABOUT GHO SAMRAKSHANAM IN DEIVATHIN KURAL

GHO MATHA - Gho means cow. Matha means mother . The cow is vatsala as it loves its calf with all its heart. Cow is so soft by nature . Thats why we call her matha "Mother".

Our mother feeds us milk only for a year or two whereas Gho Matha feeds us for our entire life time. Is it not the responsibility of the children to take care of their mother when she gets old? In the same way, it is the responsibility of every individual to feed the cows and protect the ones that has stopped milking from slaughtering says Shri Mahaperiyava.

It is said in the Vedas that all Gods, Goddess, including the Trinity has their abode in the cow. All 33 crore devathas reside in Gho Matha. It brings good fortune to those who worship it. Also worshiping mother Kamadhenu or Cow helps in cleansing our sins committed in this birth and the previous one. She is a walking temple by herself. All punniya theerthas (Sacred rivers) reside in her. Protecting Mother Cow is Protecting the world.

Cows not only gives us milk, she also provides sacred items such as curd, ghee, dried cow dung cakes that is used for Homams/Yajnams. We also get Basma (Vibuthi) from dried cow dung cakes. Gho Matha takes the form of Bhu Matha (Mother nature) and Sri Matha (Maha lakshmi thayar) as well.

The puranas, the smritis, and the dharmasastras has explicitly explained the importance of devotion towards cow.

Gosuktam saya , ‘dhenuh sadanam rayeenam’ meaning “the cow is a repository of all kinds of wealth and prosperity”. In other words, the cow is like a mother to all worldly products.

The cow has been described in Skandapurana as ‘sarvateerthamayee’. Anointing the forehead with cowdust relieves one of all sins and transgressions.

Srimad-Bhagavatam 3.16.10 says

द्रक्ष्यन्त्यघक्षतद्रुशो ह्यहिमन्यवस्तान्
ग्रुध्रा रुषा मम कुषन्त्यधिदण्डनेतु :|
ये ब्राह्मणान्मयि धिया क्षिपतोर्चयन्त:
तुष्यद्ध्रुद: स्मितसुधोक्षितपद्मवक्त्रा :

Krishna himself states, “The brahmanas, the cows, and the defenseless creatures are My own body.” So we have to understand that to please Krishna we have to serve Brahmanas and the Ghomathas

In Mahabharata it is mentioned as follows:
“nothing is unattainable for the one who worship cow. Whatever such devotees desire, they achieve. A lady who is devoted to the cow can have her desires fulfilled by the cow’s mercy. One who desires a son or a daughter, who desires wealth, one who desires piety, or knowledge
— Every one can get their desires fulfilled by the mercy of the cow.”

Benefits of Gho Pooja

  1. Offering grass, food, agathi keerai, banana to cows on mondays will cleanse our mathru, pithru dosha
  2. Offering water and food to cows on tuesdays will bless us with own house and properties.
  3. Feeding the cows on wednesdays will bless us with career upliftment
  4. Offering rice porridge to cows will remove purva jenma dosha
  5. Doing gho poojas on Fridays will shower us with the blessings of Sri Mahalakshmi
  6. Feeding grass and agathi keerai to cows on saturdays will relieve us from the shackles of poverty.
  7. On Dwadashi worshipping cow and feeding them will provide punya of annathanam
